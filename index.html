About the Artist

Dx Mercado is a multifaceted artist, athlete, filmmaker, researcher, and scholar. His passion for the sciences was solidified during his time at the Philippine Science High School. He went on to play varsity football as a midfielder and compete as a middle-distance runner for the University of the Philippines - Los Baños. He later earned a Master of Science in Criminal Justice, focusing his research on forensics. With a patent for a forensic instrument in the works, Dx may soon add "inventor" to his list of accomplishments.

Theoretically—and with a nod to modern action movies—Dx could have been one of the most skilled assassins. His solid forensic background, coupled with a Judo medal and army training, certainly gives him the tools. He completed his Limited Warfighting Competency at Camp Riego de Dios in 2019, clocking 11 minutes and 30 seconds in a 2-mile run. As a serialized member of the Armed Forces, his physical and intellectual credentials are unmatched: he is a Philippine Science Scholar, a Cum Laude graduate in his master’s program, and someone who took the University of the Cordilleras entrance exam with just one mistake, finishing the test in less than half the allotted time. Outside the academic sphere, Dx embraces adrenaline, with hobbies like Airsoft and Surfing.

Despite his diverse qualifications, Dx chose to pursue a more peaceful path, immersing himself in the arts while applying his scientific knowledge to his creative work. He has participated in over 50 group exhibitions, showcasing his work in prestigious venues such as the Cultural Center of the Philippines, Sining Makiling Gallery in Laguna, and the Ibagiw Arts Fest in Baguio City. He once contributed to a Guinness World Record for the longest painting and has curated numerous exhibitions, delivered lectures, and earned accolades in competitions. Among his achievements are winning Juror's Choice at the GSIS National Art Competition and being a finalist in the Metrobank Art and Design Excellence awards. Dx is also a filmmaker, having directed short films and secured a copyright for one of his screenplays.

In 2019, Dx briefly stepped away from the arts to focus on academics, completing both his Bachelor of Forensic Science and Master of Science in Criminal Justice in just five years. His research, which centers on crime scene reconstruction, particularly blood pattern analysis, is set to be published in high-impact journals. As he continues to blend science and creativity, Dx Mercado remains a dynamic force in both the academic and artistic worlds, always pushing the boundaries of innovation.

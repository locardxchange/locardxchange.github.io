Pillar 1: The Learner's Sandbox

Exactly three student rules written for Grade 12 learners who regularly use search engines, social media, AI tools, cloud storage, and learning management systems.

1

Think Before You Post, Share, or Forward

Do share only class-related, respectful, and verified content. Do not post classmates' photos, videos, screenshots, grades, chats, or personal information without clear permission.

Rule reminder: “Kung hindi sa'yo, huwag i-post.”
2

Use AI and Online Sources Honestly

Do use AI, websites, and digital references to study, draft, review, and improve your work. Do not copy-paste answers, hide AI use, invent sources, or submit work you cannot explain.

Rule reminder: “Gamitin ang tool, pero ikaw pa rin ang accountable.”
3

Keep School Accounts Safe

Do use strong passwords, log out from shared devices, and report suspicious links or account activity. Do not share passwords, open unknown links, or use another person's account.

Rule reminder: “Account mo, responsibilidad mo.”

Pillar 2: The Virtual Fortress

Exactly one infrastructure rule for the school ICT team to implement on campus devices and Wi-Fi.

🔐
Campus SafeSearch + Category Filtering

ICT Guardrail: Filter High-Risk Content and Monitor School-Network Misuse

The school ICT team must activate DNS or firewall-based filtering on campus Wi-Fi and school devices. The filter must block adult content, gambling sites, malware and phishing domains, illegal download pages, proxy or VPN bypass sites, and websites that promote academic cheating. The system must also log repeated attempts to access blocked categories during school hours for guidance-based intervention, not public shaming.

Blocked: pornography, gambling, malware, phishing, illegal downloads, bypass tools, and cheating services.
Monitored: repeated attempts to bypass filters, suspicious login patterns, and unsafe browsing on school-managed accounts.
Purpose: learner protection, cybersecurity, academic integrity, and responsible use of school ICT resources.

Pillar 3: The Home Bridge

Exactly one parent check that connects school expectations with daily digital wellness routines at home.

🏠
15-Minute Weekly Digital Check-In

Parent Strategy: The “Check, Talk, Reset” Routine

Once a week, parents and Grade 12 learners must spend 15 minutes reviewing school-related device use through a simple routine: check learning apps and deadlines, talk about suspicious messages or online stress, and reset boundaries for sleep, study time, and screen time. This keeps digital safety collaborative rather than punitive.

Check: LMS notifications, class group chats, school email, deadlines, and submitted tasks.
Talk: online pressure, suspicious links, cyberbullying, AI use, and privacy concerns.
Reset: device charging outside the bed area, study-first screen schedule, and no late-night academic cramming online.
